LasVegas: Tableau Dashboard

March 31, 2021

Objective: To create interactive dashboard for a client.

Dataset: The ‘LasVegas’ dataset is a Multivariate dataset that provides details on 504 TripAdvisor reviews of the 21 hotels located in the Strip, Las Vegas.

Audience: User of the dashboard created using Las Vegas dataset is Chris Brooks, manager at Wyndham Grand Desert (3-Star hotel) who is keen to perform SWOT analysis based on travelers’ feedback (review score). Chris would like to assess Wyndham’s strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats with respect to other 3-Star hotels in the region (competitors). He also would like to assess which type of travelers don’t have positive feeling towards Wyndham. Hospitality is a seasonal business; thus, Chris would like to identify which period of the year affects Wyndham’s review score and has less volume of travelers (considering number of reviews for a period as proxy for volume of travelers). Apart from review score, Chris believes that amenities provided by the hotel, rating (Star) of the hotel, and proximity of tourist attractions/restaurants from hotel location are key factors for travelers to choose best hotel on their trip. Chris is a computer literate and has better understanding of descriptive statistics for business.

Chris would like to have answers for below questions with respect to all 3-Star hotels in the region: a) Which type of customers have less positive feeling towards Wyndham Grand Desert categorized based on their native place, period of travel, and travel type (business, couples, family, friends, or solo)? b) Does amenities and external factors like proximity of tourist attractions and restaurants affect review score provided by travelers? c) Which period of the year Wyndham should focus to improve review score?

Following information is provided on dashboard to answer above questions: a) Average score and rank within 3-Star hotels. b) Proportion of traveler’s based on their native continent. c) Traveler type bifurcated based on period of travel. d) Quarterly trend in number of reviews and review score.

Overview of Wyndham’s average review score, rank, total reviews, number of tourist attractions and restaurant within 0.5 km, and proportion of travelers based on their native continent is provided so that Chris can crosscheck how Wyndham’s rank and review score changes in presence of other factors. Rank is calculated based on summation of amenities and number of tourists’ attractions & restaurants within 0.5 km of hotel’s location. This dashboard should provide insights to Chris which might need further analysis. Insights gained from this dashboard should evoke feelings of exuberance and competitiveness.

Hotel Star (3,4, and/or 5) filter controls all the charts and tables. Interaction of Traveler Continent, Traveler Type and Period of Travel with Rank and Review score is created (Refer above Figures). Also, interaction between Number of Reviews Trend, Review Score Trend, Traveler Native Continent, Traveler Type and Period of Stays is created (Refer below figure).

Interaction between Review Score trend, Traveler Native Continent, Traveler Type, and Period of Stay

  1. Motivation of Chris: a) Based on Review Score, Wyndham’s prime competitor is Marriott’s Grand Chateau Hotel. Wyndham’s customer base in concentrated by North Americans, European and Asian customers prefer Marriott than Wyndham comparatively. Chris should analyze and come up with a strategy to attract more European and Asian customers. Wyndham’s strength is customers travelling with friends in all four quarters. But Marriott’s strength lies in Business travelers comparatively. Chris should create a strategy to attract more Business travelers. Wyndham customer base is concentrated by customers traveling with family comparatively. If Marriott comes up with a strategy to attract more customer traveling with family, then it will have adverse effect on Wyndham. Chris should consider this as threat and constantly monitor Marriott’s strategy for customers traveling with family.

b) Rank represents score based on Hotel star, and external factors beyond Chris’s control such as Number of tourist attractions and restaurants within 0.5 km. It seems these external factors does not hinder customers while deciding which hotel to stay as Wyndham tops in Rank, but review score is less than Marriott in group of 3-Hotels only.

c) Wyndham’s score drops sharply in period Jun-Aug and Sep-Nov. In both the periods, Wyndham has maximum customers traveling with family and couple customers. Chris should identify which type of service/amenities these customers expect and improve on existing services.

d) Dashboard is designed to accommodate more extended comparative analysis across all the hotels and parameters.

c) Supplementary dashboard is provided for the search results available for prospective customers.
